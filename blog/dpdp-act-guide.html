DPDP Act 2023: What Every Indian Tech Startup Must Do Now

February 20, 2026
7 min read
by Sandhya Kumar

The Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act 2023 is no longer a "future concern." For Indian startups, compliance is now a prerequisite for growth and investment.

The Core Tenets of the Act

The DPDP Act introduced the concept of the "Data Fiduciary" and the "Data Principal." As a tech startup, you are likely a Data Fiduciary—responsible for how user data is collected, stored, and processed.

Unlike previous regulations, the DPDP Act is built on the principle of "explicit consent." Pre-ticked boxes and vague privacy policies are now illegal.

"Compliance isn't just a legal hurdle; it's a trust-building exercise. In a world of data breaches, privacy is your most valuable feature."

Immediate Steps for Compliance

  • Audit Your Data Streams: Map exactly where your user data comes from, who has access to it, and where it is stored.
  • Implement Granular Consent: Users must be able to consent to specific uses of their data. For example, they might consent to account creation but not to marketing emails.
  • Appoint a Data Protection Officer (DPO): Even if not strictly required by your current scale, having a dedicated point of contact for data queries is a best practice.

The Penalty for Non-Compliance

The stakes are high. Non-compliance can result in penalties of up to ₹250 Crores. For a startup, this is a business-ending event.

  1. Failure to prevent data breach: Up to ₹250 Cr.
  2. Failure to notify breach: Up to ₹200 Cr.
  3. Non-fulfillment of obligations: Up to ₹150 Cr.
